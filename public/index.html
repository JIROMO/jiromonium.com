JIROMONIUM

A material for thought.

JIROMONIUM is not an ideology.

It is not something
to believe in.

It is something that exists
before an idea takes form.

We did not create it.

We discovered it.

Not as an answer,
but as a property.

A way of seeing.

A way of questioning.

A way of making.

JIROMONIUM cannot be seen.

It can only be recognized.

Not by its appearance,
but by what it allows to happen.

It leaves room.

It invites interpretation.

It continues beyond its maker.

Principles

01

Ask why.

Nothing should exist without a reason.
Design begins by questioning what has already been accepted.

02

Do not complete everything.

Completion is only one moment.
Leave space for change, interpretation, and continuation.

03

Create conditions.

Do not decide every outcome.
Design a state from which something can begin.

04

Respect what already exists.

Do not erase the nature of things.
Observe their properties, their limits, and their possibilities.

05

Keep the parts simple.

Complexity does not need to exist inside each individual part.
It can emerge through relationships, combinations, and time.

06

Allow participation.

People are not only users.
They can alter, continue, and become part of what is made.

It is not a style.

A style can be imitated.

JIROMONIUM is not recognized
by a shape, a color, or a surface.

It is recognized by the thinking
that remains within what was made.

It is not an ideology.

An ideology asks to be followed.

JIROMONIUM asks to be used.

How it appears
depends on the person who encounters it.

Nothing is truly finished.

A thing changes through use.

An idea changes through interpretation.

A system changes through participation.

What is made continues
after it leaves the hands of its maker.

JIROMONIUM

Not a belief.
Not a style.
Not an answer.

A property found
where thought becomes form.

By JIROMO.
